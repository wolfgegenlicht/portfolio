I'm a UX/UI Designer with over 10 years of experience.

My approach? Figure out what users actually need (not what we think they need), then build something that doesn't make them want to throw their laptop out the window.

I do user research, UI/UX design and design systems. Basically, I'm the person who asks "but why would someone click that?" before anyone builds it.

I believe good design should be like a good joke: if you have to explain it, it's probably not working.

When I'm not staring at Figma, you'll find me diving into tons of books about psychology and behavioral economics (and yes, sometimes also fiction, fantasy and sci-fi books), tinkering with new AI tools, or attempting to explain to my friends why that button placement is giving me anxiety.

I'm always learning something new, whether it's a design trend or why humans do the weird things they do.

Work Philosophy

Great design isn't just about making things look pretty... It's about solving real problems for real people. My approach is rooted in understanding user needs, business objectives, and technical constraints.

What makes me different:

  • No jargon juggling: I speak in clear, understandable language
  • Focus on essentials: Not chasing trends, but solving problems
  • Collaborative approach: Working together, not performing solo
  • Love for Devs: Speaking their language so we can build great things together, not fight over impossible designs
  • Business understanding: Balancing user needs with business goals
